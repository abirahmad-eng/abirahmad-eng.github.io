// 02 — Research

Research Interests

Transportation Safety · Computer Vision · Intelligent Transportation Systems

My primary research focus is road safety and traffic conflict analysis using computer vision. For my undergraduate thesis, I am working with video footage collected from existing road cameras at intersections and road sections, training custom object detection models using YOLOv8 and Roboflow, and extracting traffic conflict parameters to better understand crash causation patterns.

Alongside this, I use Python for data processing and analysis, and plan to incorporate statistical modeling tools as the research progresses. I also work with SUMO for microsimulation, which helps me think about traffic behavior and road dynamics in a more structured way.

My broader interests span Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), mobility data science, and the application of machine learning to transportation safety problems. These are areas I genuinely want to keep exploring, and I'm looking to pursue them further through doctoral research.

// 03 — Projects

Academic Projects

Structural · Transportation · Water Resources · Design

PROJECT 01 · Nov 2025 – Mar 2026

Structural Design and Analysis of an 8-Storied Reinforced Concrete Residential Building (BNBC 2020) using ETABS

An individual academic project focused on the structural design and analysis of an 8-storied reinforced concrete residential building, carried out in compliance with the Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC 2020). The project involved modeling the complete building structure in ETABS, defining load combinations, and analyzing structural behavior under gravity and lateral loads. Member sizing, reinforcement design, and code-based checks were performed to ensure the structure met safety and serviceability requirements. This project gave me a solid hands-on understanding of how structural analysis software translates real engineering principles into a buildable design.

ETABS · MS Excel · BNBC 2020View Project

PROJECT 02 · Jun 2025 – Oct 2025

Planning and Design of Water Supply System for a Textile Industrial Village

A group academic project completed as part of the CEE 4634 Environmental Engineering Sessional 2 course. The project involved the end-to-end planning and design of a water supply system for a hypothetical 100-acre textile industrial village. Starting from scratch, we prepared a company organogram and master layout with proper roadway alignment, estimated the population, and calculated the overall water demand for the village. Based on that, we designed industrial groundwater wells, sized and located pumping stations, and developed a complete water distribution network supported by hydraulic calculations. Final drawings were produced in AutoCAD, with all calculations and data management handled in Microsoft Excel.

AutoCAD · Microsoft Excel · Industrial PlanningView Project

PROJECT 03 · Feb 2026 – Mar 2026

Pedestrian Truss Bridge Design Using CSI SAP2000

An individual academic project completed as part of the CEE 4712 Structural Analysis and Design Sessional II course. A full 3D pedestrian truss bridge model was developed in SAP2000 using A992Fy50 steel, with nine distinct member types including chords, diagonals, floor beams, stringers, bracings, and portal frames. Dead and factored pedestrian live loads were applied, and an iterative trial and error process was carried out to revise and finalize member sections until all members passed the stress and capacity check. The final design confirmed expected truss behavior, with bending moments confined to floor beams and stringers while main truss members carried pure axial forces.

SAP2000 · Truss Bridge · Pedestrian BridgeView Project

PROJECT 04 · May 2025 – Aug 2025

Integrated Load Analysis and RCC Design of a Six Story Commercial Building Following BNBC 2020

An individual academic project completed as part of the Design of Concrete Structures sessional. The project covered a full structural design workflow for a six-story reinforced concrete commercial building, following BNBC 2020 guidelines. I worked through every major structural component including slabs, staircases, beams, and columns, performing detailed vertical and lateral load calculations accounting for both wind and earthquake effects. Loads were distributed frame-wise to understand how individual members respond under combined loading conditions. ETABS was used for structural analysis, intermediate calculations were organized in Microsoft Excel, and final reinforcement detailing and construction drawings were completed in AutoCAD.

AutoCAD · MS Excel · Seismic Design · Wind Load · ETABSView Project

PROJECT 05 · August 2025

Structural Analysis and Design of a Steel Truss Bridge Using SAP2000

An individual academic project completed as part of the CEE 4614 Structural Analysis and Design Sessional 1 course. The project involved the structural analysis and design of an 11-meter steel truss bridge intended for an agricultural setting in Chittagong, a region characterized by significant wind exposure. A detailed 3D model was developed in SAP2000 with realistic support conditions and member properties. Load analysis covered dead loads including self-weight, roofing, and purlins, as well as live loads and wind loads calculated based on Chittagong's specified wind speed (80 m/s) and exposure category following BNBC 2020 provisions. Both windward and leeward wind effects were considered. Member forces were used to size and optimize the truss members for structural safety while keeping the design practical and economical.

SAP2000 · Steel Design · Truss Bridge · Bridge EngineeringView Project

PROJECT 06 · April 2023

Detailed Architectural and Structural Drawings of a Six Storied Residential Building

A group academic project completed as part of the CEE 4202 Civil Engineering Drawing II course. The project involved preparing a complete set of detailed drawings for a six storied residential building using AutoCAD. The drawing set covered floor plans for all levels including ground floor parking, first floor office space, and second to fifth floor apartment layouts. Structural drawings included column and beam layout plans, grade beam and floor beam long sections, footing layout, slab reinforcement details, stair reinforcement, column long sections with lapping and splice zone details, and beam column joint details. Each team member was responsible for specific sheets, contributing to a coordinated and complete drawing package.
