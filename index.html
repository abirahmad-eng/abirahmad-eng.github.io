// 02 — Research

Research Interests

My primary research focus is road safety and traffic conflict analysis using computer vision. For my undergraduate thesis, I am collecting real-world video footage from intersections and road sections, training custom object detection models using YOLOv8 and Roboflow, and extracting traffic conflict parameters to understand crash causation patterns.

Beyond video analytics, I work with microsimulation tools like SUMO to model traffic behavior and road dynamics. My broader interests span Intelligent Transportation Systems, mobility data science, and the application of machine learning to transportation safety problems.

These are areas I want to keep going deeper into — whether through graduate research or industry roles in transportation safety and ITS.

PROJECT 02

Comparative Statistical Analysis of Engineering Data: Manual vs. Software Validation

PROJECT 03

Planning and Design of Water Supply System for a Textile Industrial Village

Designed a comprehensive water supply system for an industrial village setting, covering population forecasting, demand estimation, distribution network layout, pipe sizing, and treatment facility planning following standard engineering codes.

Water Supply · Infrastructure Design

PROJECT 04

Integrated Load Analysis and RCC Design of a Six Storey Commercial Building Following BNBC 2020

Conducted complete structural analysis and reinforced concrete design of a six-storey commercial building, applying Bangladesh National Building Code 2020 provisions for gravity and lateral load combinations, slab, beam, column and foundation design.

Structural Engineering · BNBC 2020 · RCC Design

Structural Analysis and Design of a Steel Truss Bridge Using SAP2000

Modeled and analyzed a steel truss bridge using SAP2000 finite element software. Applied dead, live, and wind load combinations to determine member forces, stress distributions, and critical design checks for structural integrity.

SAP2000 · Bridge Engineering · Structural Analysis
